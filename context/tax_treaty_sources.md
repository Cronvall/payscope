## 2. Sweden (Skatteverket / Regeringen)

Sweden's domestic WHT on dividends to non-residents (*kupongskatt*, per Kupongskattelagen 1970:624) is **30%**. Under the Nordic multilateral treaty and most bilateral treaties the rate is typically reduced to **15%**. Refund claims use form **SKV 3740** (or SKV 3742 for Swiss residents) and must be filed within **5 years** of the dividend payment. Relief-at-source is available as an alternative to refund claims.

## 8. Germany (BZSt / BMF)

Germany's effective WHT is **26.375%** (25% + 5.5% solidarity surcharge). Treaty rates are commonly **15%** (portfolio) or **5%/0%** (qualifying corporate shareholders). Refund applications to the BZSt must be filed electronically since 2023 and within **4 years**. Processing times currently exceed **18 months**.

## 9. France (DGFiP / impots.gouv.fr)

France's WHT on dividends to non-resident individuals is **12.8%**; to non-resident companies **25%**; punitive **75%** rate for non-cooperative jurisdictions. Treaty rates commonly reduce to **15%** or lower. Forms **5000** (certificate of residence) and **5001** (dividend reclaim) are required. Claims must generally be filed by 31 December of the second year following the year the tax was paid.
